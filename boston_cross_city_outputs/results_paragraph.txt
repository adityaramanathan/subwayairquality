Using cleaned MBTA Red, Orange, and Blue line headways from 2024-2025, the model estimated Boston expected waits of 3.9 minutes at the mean, 4.87 minutes at P75, and 6.35 minutes at P90. With Boston underground PM2.5 set to 327 μg/m³ and IR=10 L/min, the corresponding doses were 12.76, 15.91, and 20.76 μg, producing EDF values of 1.84, 2.29, and 2.99 relative to the NYC reference dose of 6.95 μg. The P90 result means that a long-waiting Boston commuter in the cleaned MBTA data receives 20.76 μg in this platform-wait component, or 2.99 times the NYC reference scenario.
